I am a postdoctoral associate at the Computer Science and Artifical Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL) of MIT working under the supervision of Michael Stonebraker and Samuel Madden. I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Purdue University under the supervision of Walid Aref and Mourad Ouzzani. My research interests revolve around data management in general and data quality in particular.

Data scientists spend most of their time doing "grunt" work, i.e., discovery, preparing and cleaning the data. The goal of my research is to make building data science pipelines "fun" by developing systems that aid in (1) Data debugging; (2) Data preparation and (3) Data discovery. In collaboration with industrial parties (e.g., Intel, Massachusetts General Hospital), my systems target real-world use cases.
